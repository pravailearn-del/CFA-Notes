LOS 7.b Coming Soon
Describe exchange rate regimes and explain the effects of exchange rates on countries' international trade and capital flows
Compare exchange rate regimes on spectrum from fixed to floating. Fixed/Pegged: currency tied to another currency or basket, requires reserves and capital controls. Crawling peg: gradual, predetermined adjustments. Managed float: mostly market-determined with occasional intervention. Free float: purely market-determined. Analyze effects on trade: currency appreciation makes exports more expensive (reduces competitiveness) and imports cheaper (increases import demand). Effects on capital flows: appreciation attracts foreign investment due to expected returns; depreciation may trigger capital outflows. Understand J-curve effect: trade balance initially worsens after depreciation (existing contracts) before improving (volume effects). Consider trade-offs: fixed rates provide certainty but limit monetary policy flexibility; floating rates provide policy autonomy but create uncertainty.

LOS 7.c Coming Soon
Describe common objectives of capital restrictions imposed by governments
Understand capital controls (restrictions on cross-border capital flows) and their objectives. Preventing sudden capital flight and financial crises by limiting speculative flows that can destabilize markets. Maintaining fixed exchange rate regimes by preventing arbitrage that would drain reserves. Reducing vulnerability to external shocks and contagion from global financial markets. Preserving monetary policy independence under fixed exchange rates (preventing capital flows from forcing rate changes). Tax base protection by limiting tax avoidance through offshore movement. Supporting infant industries by preventing foreign takeovers. Analyze types: inflow controls (taxes on foreign investment, minimum holding periods), outflow controls (restrictions on domestic residents investing abroad), and dual exchange rate systems. Consider costs: reduced market efficiency, administrative burden, potential for evasion, and signals of economic weakness.
