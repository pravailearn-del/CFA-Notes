Company Analysis: Past and Present

Module Overview: This module focuses on analyzing a company's historical and current performance through examination of business models, revenue drivers, profitability metrics, and capital structure. Essential for fundamental analysis and equity valuation.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 5.a Coming Soon
Describe the elements that should be covered in a thorough company research report
Learn the comprehensive structure of a research report: executive summary, business overview and strategy, industry analysis, competitive positioning, financial analysis (historical and forecasted), valuation, investment thesis, risks, and recommendation. Understand the importance of addressing management quality, corporate governance, ESG factors, and catalysts. Learn how to present information in a clear, logical, and actionable format for investment decision-making.

LOS 5.b Coming Soon
Determine a company's business model
Understand a business model as the way a company creates, delivers, and captures value. Analyze key components: customer value proposition (what needs are being met), revenue model (how the company makes money), cost structure (major cost drivers), key resources and capabilities, and distribution channels. Distinguish between different business model types: subscription, marketplace, freemium, razor-and-blade, franchise, and others. Evaluate business model sustainability and competitive advantages.

LOS 5.c Coming Soon
Evaluate a company's revenue and revenue drivers, including pricing power
Learn to decompose revenue into volume and price components. Analyze revenue drivers: market growth, market share changes, pricing actions, new product introductions, and geographic expansion. Understand pricing power indicators: brand strength, product differentiation, switching costs, and competitive dynamics. Evaluate revenue quality by examining sustainability, diversification, and organic vs. acquired growth. Analyze segment reporting to understand revenue composition and growth trends across different business lines.

LOS 5.d Coming Soon
Evaluate a company's operating profitability and working capital using key measures
Master profitability metrics: gross profit margin (revenue - COGS) / revenue, operating margin (EBIT / revenue), and net profit margin (net income / revenue). Analyze working capital efficiency using: days sales outstanding (DSO), days inventory outstanding (DIO), days payables outstanding (DPO), and the cash conversion cycle (DSO + DIO - DPO). Understand how operating leverage, economies of scale, and cost management affect profitability. Evaluate trends and compare to industry peers and historical performance.

LOS 5.e Coming Soon
Evaluate a company's capital investments and capital structure
Analyze capital expenditure (CapEx) patterns and their relationship to depreciation (maintenance vs. growth CapEx). Evaluate return on invested capital (ROIC) to assess capital allocation efficiency. Examine capital structure through debt-to-equity ratio, debt-to-assets ratio, and interest coverage ratios. Understand financial leverage effects on ROE (DuPont analysis). Assess capital allocation decisions: internal investments, acquisitions, dividends, and share buybacks. Evaluate whether capital structure supports business strategy and maintains financial flexibility.
