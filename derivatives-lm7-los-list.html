Pricing and Valuation of Interest Rates and Other Swaps

Module Overview: This module examines swap contracts, particularly interest rate swaps and their relationship to forward contracts. You'll learn how swaps can be viewed as a series of forwards, understand the distinction between swap value and swap price, and master the fundamentals of swap pricing and valuation.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 7.a Coming Soon
Describe how swap contracts are similar to but different from a series of forward contracts
Understand swaps as series of forward contracts with key similarities and differences. Similarities: both involve future exchanges at predetermined prices/rates, both are customizable OTC instruments, both can be used for hedging or speculation. Key difference: in a series of independent forward contracts, each contract has its own forward price/rate based on the forward curve; in a swap, all exchanges use the same fixed rate (the swap fixed rate). For example, in an interest rate swap, the fixed rate is a weighted average of the forward rates, designed so the swap has zero initial value. This makes swaps simpler to execute and manage than a portfolio of individual forwards.

LOS 7.b Coming Soon
Contrast the value and price of swaps
Distinguish between swap price and swap value. Swap price (fixed rate): the fixed interest rate agreed upon at initiation, set so the swap has zero initial value; calculated as the rate that equates the present value of fixed payments to the present value of expected floating payments. Swap value: the market value of the swap during its life; for the fixed-rate payer, value = PV(floating leg) - PV(fixed leg). At initiation, value = 0 for both parties. During life, value changes as interest rates move‚Äîif rates rise, the fixed-rate payer benefits (positive value) as they're paying below-market rates. Value also changes as time passes and payments are made.
