Working Capital and Liquidity

Module Overview: This module focuses on the management of short-term assets and liabilities. Understanding the cash conversion cycle, liquidity assessment, and working capital optimization is critical for evaluating a company's operational efficiency and short-term financial health.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 4.a Coming Soon
Explain the cash conversion cycle and compare issuers' cash conversion cycles
Master the cash conversion cycle (CCC) calculation: CCC = Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) + Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) - Days Payables Outstanding (DPO). Understand that a shorter cycle indicates faster conversion of investment into cash. Calculate DIO = (Average Inventory / COGS) √ó 365, DSO = (Average Receivables / Revenue) √ó 365, and DPO = (Average Payables / COGS) √ó 365. Compare cycles across companies and industries, recognizing that optimal cycles vary by business model. Analyze how changes in each component affect liquidity and working capital needs.

LOS 4.b Coming Soon
Explain liquidity and compare issuers' liquidity levels
Understand liquidity as the ability to meet short-term obligations without significant loss. Calculate and interpret liquidity ratios: Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities (measures ability to pay short-term debts), Quick Ratio = (Cash + Marketable Securities + Receivables) / Current Liabilities (more stringent, excludes inventory), and Cash Ratio = (Cash + Marketable Securities) / Current Liabilities (most conservative). Compare ratios across companies, considering industry norms and business models. Analyze primary and secondary sources of liquidity including operating cash flow, short-term investments, credit facilities, and asset sales.

LOS 4.c Coming Soon
Describe issuers' objectives and compare methods for managing working capital and liquidity
Examine working capital management objectives: maintaining adequate liquidity, minimizing costs, optimizing cash conversion cycle, and balancing profitability with liquidity. Compare management approaches for receivables (credit terms, collection policies, factoring, securitization), inventory (JIT systems, EOQ models, vendor-managed inventory), and payables (stretching payment terms while maintaining supplier relationships, early payment discounts). Analyze short-term financing options including lines of credit, commercial paper, banker's acceptances, and factoring. Understand trade-offs between conservative (high liquidity, lower returns) and aggressive (low liquidity, higher returns) working capital strategies.
