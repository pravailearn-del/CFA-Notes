Question 1 Easy

Question 6 Easy
Which measure of return is most appropriate for forecasting the expected return for the next single period?
A
Geometric mean
B
Arithmetic mean
C
Harmonic mean

‚úì Correct Answer: B (Arithmetic mean)

Explanation:

The arithmetic mean is the most appropriate measure for forecasting the expected return for the next single period because it represents the simple average of past returns.

Why not the others?

  • Geometric mean: Best for measuring historical multi-period performance and compound growth rates, not for forecasting a single future period.
  • Harmonic mean: Appropriate for cost averaging scenarios and averaging ratios, not for return forecasting.

Remember: Use arithmetic mean for single-period forecasts, geometric mean for multi-period historical analysis.

Question 8 Medium
Which of the following statements about the relationship between arithmetic and geometric means is MOST accurate?

‚úì Correct Answer: B (The arithmetic mean is greater than or equal to the geometric mean)

Explanation:

The correct relationship is: Arithmetic Mean ‚â• Geometric Mean

The two means are equal only when all the returns in the series are identical. In all other cases, the arithmetic mean will be strictly greater than the geometric mean.

Example 1: Returns all equal (10%, 10%, 10%)

Key Point: The difference between the two means increases with the variability (volatility) of returns.

Question 9 Hard

‚Ä¢ Arithmetic mean: 12%

‚Ä¢ Geometric mean: 10.42%

‚Ä¢ This includes a negative year!

Key Takeaway: The divergence between arithmetic and geometric means is a direct indicator of return volatility, not the sign of returns.
