Forward Commitment and Contingent Claim Features and Instruments

Module Overview: This module explores the major types of derivative instruments, distinguishing between forward commitments (forwards, futures, swaps) and contingent claims (options, credit derivatives). You'll learn the defining characteristics of each instrument type and understand how to calculate payoffs and profits from option positions.

Define forward contracts, futures contracts, swaps, options (calls and puts), and credit derivatives and compare their basic characteristics
Learn the fundamental features of each derivative type: forward contracts (OTC, customized, bilateral agreements), futures contracts (exchange-traded, standardized, daily settlement), swaps (series of forward contracts exchanging cash flows), call options (right to buy), put options (right to sell), and credit derivatives (instruments transferring credit risk). Compare their standardization, liquidity, counterparty risk, and typical uses.

LOS 2.b Coming Soon
Determine the value at expiration and profit from a long or a short position in a call or put option
Master the calculation of option payoffs at expiration. For call options: long call profit = max(0, S - X) - premium; short call profit = premium - max(0, S - X). For put options: long put profit = max(0, X - S) - premium; short put profit = premium - max(0, X - S). Where S is spot price at expiration, X is strike price. Understand payoff diagrams and break-even points for all four positions.

LOS 2.c Coming Soon
Contrast forward commitments with contingent claims
Understand the fundamental difference between forward commitments and contingent claims. Forward commitments (forwards, futures, swaps) create obligations for both parties to transact at a future date, with symmetric payoff profiles. Contingent claims (options, credit derivatives) provide rights but not obligations, with asymmetric payoffs‚Äîlimited downside for buyers (premium paid) but potentially unlimited upside, while sellers face limited upside (premium received) and potentially large downside risk.
