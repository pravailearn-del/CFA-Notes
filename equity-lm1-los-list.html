Market Organization and Structure

Module Overview: This module covers the fundamental functions of the financial system, classifications of assets and markets, types of securities and their characteristics, and the organization and functioning of securities markets.

by Larry Harris, PhD, CFA. Larry Harris, PhD, CFA, is at the USC Marshall School of Business (USA).

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 1.a Coming Soon
Explain the main functions of the financial system
Understand the six primary functions of the financial system: helping people achieve their purposes in using the financial system, determining rates of return, allocating capital efficiently, discovering prices, providing liquidity, and facilitating risk management. Learn how these functions work together to support economic growth and individual financial goals.

Compare positions an investor can take in an asset
Understand the difference between long positions (owning an asset with expectation of price increase) and short positions (selling borrowed assets with expectation of price decrease). Learn about the risks, potential returns, and margin requirements associated with each position type. Analyze how leverage amplifies both gains and losses.

LOS 1.g Coming Soon
Compare execution, validity, and clearing instructions
Learn about execution instructions (market orders, limit orders, stop orders), validity instructions (day orders, good-till-cancelled, immediate-or-cancel, fill-or-kill), and clearing instructions (cash vs. margin settlement). LOS 1.h Coming Soon
Compare market orders with limit orders
Understand the key differences between market orders (executed immediately at best available price, providing certainty of execution but not price) and limit orders (executed only at specified price or better, providing price certainty but not execution certainty). Analyze the trade-offs between execution speed and price control.

LOS 1.i Coming Soon
Define primary and secondary markets and explain how secondary markets support primary markets
Learn the distinction between primary markets (where securities are initially issued and capital is raised) and secondary markets (where existing securities are traded). Understand how liquid secondary markets enhance the ability of issuers to raise capital in primary markets by providing investors with exit opportunities and price discovery.

LOS 1.j Coming Soon
Describe how securities, contracts, and currencies are traded in quote-driven, order-driven, and brokered markets
Explore the three main market structures: quote-driven markets (dealers quote bid and ask prices), order-driven markets (buyers and sellers submit orders that are matched electronically or through auction), and brokered markets (brokers arrange trades between buyers and sellers, often for unique or illiquid assets). Understand the advantages and typical use cases for each structure.

LOS 1.k Coming Soon
Describe characteristics of a well-functioning financial system
Learn the seven key characteristics of well-functioning financial systems: complete markets (wide range of instruments available), operational efficiency (low transaction costs), informational efficiency (prices reflect available information), allocational efficiency (capital flows to best uses), timely and accurate price discovery, liquidity, and the ability to support innovation. Understand why these characteristics matter for economic growth.

Describe objectives of market regulation

Learn how regulators balance these objectives and the trade-offs involved in regulatory design.
