Company Analysis: Forecasting

Module Overview: This module focuses on techniques for projecting a company's future financial performance. Essential skill for equity valuation and investment decision-making.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 7.a Coming Soon
Explain principles and approaches to forecasting a company's financial results and position
Learn forecasting principles: build from the top down (start with macroeconomic and industry factors, then company-specific), use both historical analysis and forward-looking judgment, maintain internal consistency across financial statements, and iterate to ensure reasonable assumptions. Understand forecasting approaches: trend extrapolation (historical patterns), regression analysis (statistical relationships), scenario analysis (multiple possible outcomes), and qualitative factors (management guidance, industry dynamics). Balance mechanical projections with business judgment.

Explain approaches to forecasting a company's revenues
Master revenue forecasting methods: growth rate approach (apply historical or analyst growth rates), market approach (market size √ó market share √ó price), segment approach (forecast each business line separately), and bottom-up approach (units √ó price for each product). Understand drivers to analyze: macroeconomic factors (GDP growth, consumer spending), industry trends (market growth, competitive dynamics), company-specific factors (new products, pricing actions, market share changes, geographic expansion). Consider seasonality and cyclicality in revenue patterns.

LOS 7.c Coming Soon
Explain approaches to forecasting a company's operating expenses and working capital
Learn to forecast operating expenses: fixed vs. variable cost classification, percentage of sales method (COGS, SG&A as % of revenue based on historical ratios), analyzing operating leverage effects, and considering economies/diseconomies of scale. Forecast working capital components: accounts receivable using DSO (days sales outstanding), inventory using DIO (days inventory outstanding), accounts payable using DPO (days payables outstanding). Understand cash conversion cycle dynamics and working capital needs for growth. Consider industry practices and company-specific changes in efficiency.

LOS 7.d Coming Soon
Explain approaches to forecasting a company's capital investments and capital structure
Forecast capital expenditures (CapEx): separate maintenance CapEx (replace existing assets, often approximated by depreciation) from growth CapEx (expand capacity, new initiatives). Use CapEx-to-sales ratio, CapEx-to-depreciation ratio, or project-specific analysis. Forecast capital structure changes: model debt issuance/repayment, share issuance/buybacks, and dividend payments. Understand target capital structure and financial policies. Ensure forecast maintains financial viability (adequate liquidity, reasonable leverage ratios) and aligns with management's stated strategy.

LOS 7.e Coming Soon
Describe the use of scenario analysis in forecasting
Understand scenario analysis as developing multiple coherent forecasts based on different assumptions about key drivers. Learn to construct scenarios: base case (most likely outcome), bull case (optimistic assumptions), and bear case (pessimistic assumptions). Each scenario should have internally consistent assumptions about revenue growth, margins, capital requirements, and external factors. Assign probabilities to scenarios to calculate expected values. Use scenario analysis to assess valuation sensitivity, downside risk, and the range of potential outcomes. Understand limitations: scenarios may not capture all possibilities, probability estimation is subjective.
