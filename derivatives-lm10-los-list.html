Describe the concept of risk neutrality in derivatives pricing
Understand risk neutrality as a mathematical framework, not an assumption about investor behavior. Risk-neutral pricing means we can value derivatives as if: (1) all investors are risk neutral (indifferent to risk), (2) the expected return on all assets equals the risk-free rate, and (3) future cash flows can be discounted at the risk-free rate. This approach works because derivative prices are determined by arbitrage and replication, not by investor risk preferences. The risk-neutral probabilities (œÄ) are not actual probabilities of up/down moves‚Äîthey're artificial probabilities that make the expected return on the stock equal the risk-free rate. This elegant framework simplifies valuation while still producing correct market prices that prevent arbitrage, regardless of investors' actual risk attitudes.
