The Firm and Market Structures

Module Overview: This module analyzes different market structures and their impact on firm behavior. Understanding production costs, breakeven analysis, and the characteristics of perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, and monopoly.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 1.a Coming Soon
Determine and interpret breakeven and shutdown points of production, as well as how economies and diseconomies of scale affect costs under perfect and imperfect competition
Master the calculation of breakeven points (where TR = TC) and shutdown points (where P < AVC). Understand economies of scale (decreasing LRAC as output increases) through specialization, bulk purchasing, and technology adoption. Analyze diseconomies of scale from coordination problems and bureaucracy. Compare cost structures across perfect and imperfect competition markets.

LOS 1.b Coming Soon
Describe characteristics of perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, and pure monopoly
Understand the four market structures: Perfect competition (many firms, homogeneous products, no barriers, price takers), monopolistic competition (many firms, differentiated products, low barriers), oligopoly (few firms, interdependence, high barriers, game theory), and pure monopoly (single firm, unique product, prohibitive barriers, price maker). Analyze barriers to entry and market power in each structure.

LOS 1.c Coming Soon
Explain supply and demand relationships under monopolistic competition, including the optimal price and output for firms as well as pricing strategy
Analyze monopolistic competition where firms face downward-sloping demand curves due to product differentiation. Determine optimal output where MR = MC and optimal price on the demand curve above this point. Understand long-run equilibrium where economic profit = 0 due to low entry barriers. Explore pricing strategies including branding, advertising, and product differentiation to maintain market power.

LOS 1.d Coming Soon
Explain supply and demand relationships under oligopoly, including the optimal price and output for firms as well as pricing strategy
Examine oligopoly dynamics where firm decisions are interdependent. Analyze the kinked demand curve model explaining price rigidity. Study game theory concepts including Nash equilibrium, dominant strategies, and prisoner's dilemma. Understand pricing strategies: collusion and cartels, price leadership, non-price competition. Evaluate Cournot (quantity competition) and Bertrand (price competition) models for determining optimal output and pricing.

LOS 1.e Coming Soon
Identify the type of market structure within which a firm operates and describe the use and limitations of concentration measures
Learn to identify market structures using concentration measures: N-firm concentration ratio (sum of market shares of top N firms) and Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI = sum of squared market shares). Understand HHI interpretation: < 1,000 (competitive), 1,000-1,800 (moderate concentration), > 1,800 (high concentration). Recognize limitations: geographic market definition, product substitution, potential competition, and barriers to entry not captured by concentration ratios.
