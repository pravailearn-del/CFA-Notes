Question 1 Easy
A zero-coupon bond with a face value of ‚Çπ10,000 is currently trading at ‚Çπ8,500. The bond matures in 3 years. What is the bond's implied annual return?
A. 4.35%
B. 5.56%
C. 5.73%

‚úì Correct Answer: C. 5.73%

Solution:

Using the discount bond formula: r = (FV / PV)^(1/t) - 1

r = (10,000 / 8,500)^(1/3) - 1

r = (1.1765)^(0.3333) - 1

r = 1.0573 - 1 = 0.0573 = 5.73%

Key Insight: For discount bonds, the implied return is simply the compound annual growth rate from PV to FV. The larger the discount (lower the price), the higher the implied return.

Question 7 Medium

B. 5.50%

‚úì Correct Answer: A. 5.00%

0.075 - g = 0.025

Interpretation: The index's P/E of 22 with a 55% payout ratio implies that investors expect 5% dividend growth if they require 7.5% returns. This can be evaluated against:

‚Ä¢ Historical index dividend growth

‚Ä¢ Expected nominal GDP growth

‚Ä¢ Corporate earnings growth forecasts

If 5% growth seems too optimistic, the index might be overvalued at a P/E of 22.
