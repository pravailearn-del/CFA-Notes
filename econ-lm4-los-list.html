Monetary Policy

Module Overview: This module covers central bank roles, monetary policy tools, and their economic effects. Understanding how central banks influence money supply, interest rates, inflation, and exchange rates to achieve economic objectives.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 4.a Coming Soon
Describe the roles and objectives of central banks
Understand central bank primary objectives: price stability (controlling inflation), sustainable economic growth, and full employment. Examine additional roles: lender of last resort during financial crises, banking system regulator and supervisor, manager of foreign exchange reserves and government's banker. Analyze the independence of central banks from political influence and its importance for credibility. Compare single mandate (price stability only, like ECB) versus dual mandate (price stability and maximum employment, like Federal Reserve) frameworks.

LOS 4.b Coming Soon
Describe tools used to implement monetary policy tools and the monetary transmission mechanism, and explain the relationships between monetary policy and economic growth, inflation, interest, and exchange rates
Examine monetary policy tools: Open market operations (buying/selling government securities), policy rate adjustments (discount rate, federal funds rate), reserve requirements, and quantitative easing (large-scale asset purchases). Understand the transmission mechanism: policy rate changes ‚Üí money market rates ‚Üí bond yields ‚Üí asset prices and exchange rates ‚Üí aggregate demand ‚Üí output and inflation. Analyze relationships: expansionary policy (lower rates) stimulates growth but may increase inflation and depreciate currency; contractionary policy (higher rates) controls inflation but may slow growth and appreciate currency. Consider time lags in policy effectiveness.

LOS 4.c Coming Soon
Describe qualities of effective central banks; contrast their use of inflation, interest rate, and exchange rate targeting in expansionary or contractionary monetary policy; and describe the limitations of monetary policy
Identify qualities of effective central banks: independence, credibility, transparency, and clear communication. Compare targeting regimes: Inflation targeting (explicit inflation goal, most common), interest rate targeting (managing policy rates), and exchange rate targeting (pegging currency to another). Understand expansionary policy (lower rates, increase money supply) and contractionary policy (raise rates, reduce money supply). Recognize limitations: zero lower bound problem (rates can't go significantly negative), liquidity trap (banks hoard reserves), long and variable lags, inability to address supply shocks, and limited effectiveness when fiscal policy conflicts.

LOS 4.d Coming Soon
Explain the interaction of monetary and fiscal policy
Analyze policy coordination scenarios: Complementary policies (both expansionary during recession or both contractionary during boom) reinforce each other effectively. Conflicting policies (expansionary fiscal with contractionary monetary, or vice versa) create uncertainty and reduce effectiveness. Understand fiscal dominance when government deficits force monetary accommodation despite inflation concerns. Examine the challenge of coordinating independent institutions with different time horizons and objectives. Consider the "policy mix" for optimal outcomes: tight fiscal and loose monetary policy may support growth with lower inflation; loose fiscal and tight monetary policy may crowd out private investment.
