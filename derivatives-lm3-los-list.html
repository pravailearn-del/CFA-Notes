Derivative Benefits, Risks, and Issuer and Investor Uses

Module Overview: This module examines the practical applications of derivatives in financial markets, exploring both the advantages they offer (such as risk management, price discovery, and leverage) and the risks they present (including counterparty risk, liquidity risk, and operational complexity). Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 3.a Coming Soon
Describe benefits and risks of derivative instruments
Examine the key benefits of derivatives: risk management and hedging capabilities, price discovery and market efficiency, operational efficiency (lower transaction costs than underlying), enhanced liquidity, and leverage opportunities. Understand the associated risks: counterparty risk (especially in OTC markets), market risk from leverage amplification, liquidity risk during stressed conditions, operational and legal risks, and potential for destabilization during market crises. Recognize how derivatives can be used for both hedging and speculation.

LOS 3.b Coming Soon
Compare the use of derivatives among issuers and investors
Contrast how different market participants employ derivatives. Issuers (corporations, governments) use derivatives primarily for hedging: managing interest rate risk on debt, foreign exchange risk on international operations, and commodity price risk. Investors use derivatives for: portfolio hedging (protecting against market declines), gaining exposure to asset classes (synthetic positions), enhancing returns through leverage, implementing tactical asset allocation strategies, and arbitrage opportunities. Understand that while issuers typically focus on risk reduction, investors may use derivatives for both risk management and return enhancement.
