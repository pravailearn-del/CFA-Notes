Time Value of Money in Fixed Income & Equity

Comprehensive technical reference for exam preparation

Learning Outcome Statement

Calculate and interpret the present value (PV) of fixed-income and equity instruments based on expected future cash flows.

I. Core Time Value Concepts

Fundamental Equations

FVt = PV(1 + r)t

Future value with discrete compounding

PV = FVt / (1 + r)t

Present value (discounting)

II. Fixed-Income Instruments

A. Discount Bonds (Zero-Coupon)

PV = FVt / (1 + r)t

Single payment at maturity; difference (FV - PV) = total interest

Example: India 20-Year Strip at 6.70% YTM

PV = INR 100 / (1.067)20 = INR 27.33

If YTM rises to 7.70%: PV = INR 22.68 (demonstrates inverse price-yield relationship)

B. Coupon Bonds

PV = Œ£ [PMTi/(1+r)i] + FVn/(1+r)n

Price-Yield Relationships:

  • Coupon Rate = YTM ‚Üí Par (PV = 100)
  • Coupon Rate > YTM ‚Üí Premium (PV > 100)
  • Coupon Rate < YTM ‚Üí Discount (PV < 100)

Example: Greece 7-Year Bond, 2% Coupon

At issuance (YTM = 2%): PV = EUR 100.00

After 2 years (YTM = 3.532%, 5 years left): PV = EUR 93.09

Capital loss: EUR 6.91 due to interest rate increase

C. Perpetual Bonds

PV = PMT / r

Infinite coupon payments, no maturity

D. Annuities (Level Payments)

Equal periodic payments combining interest and principal

III. Equity Instruments

A. Constant Dividend (Perpetuity)

PV = D / r

Used for preferred stock with fixed dividends

B. Constant Growth (Gordon Growth Model)

PV = D1 / (r - g) = D0(1 + g) / (r - g)

Constraint: r > g

Dividends grow at constant rate g perpetually

Example: Shipline PLC

D0 = GBP 1.50, g = 6%, r = 15%

D1 = 1.50 √ó 1.06 = GBP 1.59

PV = 1.59 / (0.15 - 0.06) = GBP 17.67

Compare to zero growth: GBP 10.00 (growth adds 76.7%)

C. Two-Stage Growth Model

PV = Œ£[Dt(1+gs)i/(1+r)i] + E(Sn)/(1+r)n

Terminal value: E(Sn) = Dn+1/(r - gl)

Example: Shipline Two-Stage

Stage 1 (3 years at 6%): PV = GBP 3.83

Stage 2 (perpetual at 2%): Terminal PV = GBP 9.22

Total Stock Price: GBP 13.05

Key Exam Concepts

  • Inverse Relationship: Bond prices and yields move opposite directions
  • Compounding Frequency: More frequent = lower PV for same nominal rate
  • Perpetuity: PV = Payment / Rate (bonds and stocks)
  • Growth Impact: Higher g significantly increases stock valuations
  • Annuity Behavior: Interest ‚Üì and Principal ‚Üë over time
