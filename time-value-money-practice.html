Question 1 Easy
An investor deposits INR 50,000 in a fixed deposit account that pays 7% interest compounded annually. What will be the value of the investment after 5 years?
A. INR 68,500
B. INR 70,128
C. INR 72,000

‚úì Correct Answer: B

Calculation:

FV = PV(1 + r)^t = 50,000 √ó (1.07)^5

FV = 50,000 √ó 1.40255 = INR 70,128

Key Concept: Use the future value formula with compound interest. The investment grows by 7% each year on the accumulated balance.

Question 2 Easy
Which of the following statements about the relationship between bond prices and yields is most accurate?
A. Bond prices and yields move in the same direction
B. Bond prices and yields have an inverse relationship
C. Bond prices are unaffected by changes in yields

‚úì Correct Answer: B

Explanation: Bond prices and yields have an inverse relationship. When market interest rates (yields) increase, bond prices decrease. Conversely, when yields decrease, bond prices increase.

Why? If new bonds offer higher yields, existing bonds with lower coupons become less attractive, so their prices must fall to compensate investors.

Question 3 Medium
A zero-coupon bond with a face value of USD 1,000 matures in 10 years. If the required yield is 6% per annum, what is the current price of the bond?
A. USD 558.39
B. USD 600.00
C. USD 744.09

‚úì Correct Answer: A

Calculation:

PV = FV / (1 + r)^t = 1,000 / (1.06)^10

PV = 1,000 / 1.7908 = USD 558.39

Concept: Zero-coupon bonds are sold at a discount. The difference between purchase price and face value represents the interest earned.

Question 4 Medium
A bond with a 5% annual coupon rate, EUR 100 face value, and 3 years to maturity is priced at EUR 95. The coupon is paid annually. What is the relationship between the coupon rate and yield-to-maturity?
A. Coupon rate equals YTM
B. Coupon rate is greater than YTM
C. Coupon rate is less than YTM

‚úì Correct Answer: C

Explanation: The bond is trading at a discount (EUR 95 < EUR 100 face value). When a bond trades at a discount, the yield-to-maturity is greater than the coupon rate.

Remember:

  • Discount (Price < 100): YTM > Coupon Rate
  • Par (Price = 100): YTM = Coupon Rate
  • Premium (Price > 100): YTM < Coupon Rate
Question 5 Medium

B. GBP 25.00
C. GBP 30.00

Calculation:

PV = D / r = 3.00 / 0.12 = GBP 25.00

Concept: Use the perpetuity formula for constant cash flows that continue forever. This applies to both perpetual bonds and preferred stocks with fixed dividends.

Question 7 Medium
An investor takes a 30-year mortgage loan of INR 50,00,000 at an annual interest rate of 9%. If payments are made monthly, what is the approximate monthly payment?
A. INR 40,252
B. INR 42,500
C. INR 45,000

‚úì Correct Answer: A

Calculation:

Monthly rate (r) = 9% / 12 = 0.75% = 0.0075

Number of payments (t) = 30 √ó 12 = 360

A = [r √ó PV] / [1 - (1 + r)^-t]

A = [0.0075 √ó 5,000,000] / [1 - (1.0075)^-360]

A = 37,500 / 0.9313 = INR 40,252

Note: In the early years, most of the payment goes toward interest; later, more goes toward principal.

Question 8 Hard
A bond pays semiannual coupons of EUR 4 and has a face value of EUR 100. It matures in 4 years. If the bond is currently priced at EUR 102, which statement is most accurate?
A. The bond's YTM is approximately 8%
B. The bond's YTM is less than 8%
C. The bond's YTM is greater than 8%

‚úì Correct Answer: B

Analysis:

Annual coupon = EUR 4 √ó 2 = EUR 8

Coupon rate = 8 / 100 = 8%

The bond trades at a premium (102 > 100), meaning YTM < Coupon Rate

Therefore, YTM < 8%

Remember: Premium bonds have YTM below the coupon rate because investors are paying more than face value.

Question 9 Easy
What is the present value of USD 10,000 to be received in 6 years if the discount rate is 5% compounded annually?
A. USD 7,462
B. USD 8,000
C. USD 9,524

‚úì Correct Answer: A

Calculation:

PV = FV / (1 + r)^t = 10,000 / (1.05)^6

PV = 10,000 / 1.3401 = USD 7,462

Concept: Discounting converts future cash flows to present value. The longer the time period and higher the discount rate, the lower the present value.

Question 11 Medium
If the compounding frequency increases while the stated annual rate remains constant, what happens to the effective annual rate (EAR)?
A. EAR decreases
B. EAR remains constant
C. EAR increases

‚úì Correct Answer: C

Explanation: More frequent compounding means interest is calculated and added to the principal more often, allowing you to earn "interest on interest" more frequently.

Example: 12% compounded:

  • Annually: EAR = 12.00%
  • Semiannually: EAR = 12.36%
  • Monthly: EAR = 12.68%
  • Daily: EAR = 12.75%
Question 12 Easy
Which type of bond provides no periodic interest payments and is sold at a deep discount to its face value?
A. Coupon bond
B. Zero-coupon bond
C. Perpetual bond

‚úì Correct Answer: B

Explanation: A zero-coupon bond (or discount bond) makes no periodic interest payments. Instead, it is sold at a significant discount to face value, and the investor receives the full face value at maturity.

Example: Buy at INR 27.33, receive INR 100 at maturity. The INR 72.67 difference is your interest.
