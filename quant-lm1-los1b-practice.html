Question 6

Explanation:

Fundamental Principle: For a given arithmetic mean, higher volatility results in a LOWER geometric mean. This is the "volatility drag" effect.

Why? Large losses require even larger gains to recover. A 50% loss needs a 100% gain to break even. The geometric mean accounts for this asymmetric impact.

Example with same 8% arithmetic mean:

Low Volatility (7%, 8%, 9%):
Geometric Mean = ¬≥‚àö(1.07 √ó 1.08 √ó 1.09) - 1 ‚âà 8.00%

High Volatility (-10%, 16%, 18%):

Conclusion: Fund A (stable) will have the highest geometric mean, Fund B (moderate volatility) will be in the middle, and Fund C (high volatility) will have the lowest geometric mean, despite all having 8% arithmetic mean.
