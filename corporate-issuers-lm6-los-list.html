Capital Structure

Module Overview: This module examines how companies finance their operations through different combinations of debt and equity. Understanding the weighted-average cost of capital, capital structure theories, and factors influencing financing decisions is essential for evaluating a firm's financial risk and value.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

Calculate and interpret the weighted-average cost of capital for a company
Master WACC calculation: WACC = (w_d √ó r_d √ó (1-t)) + (w_p √ó r_p) + (w_e √ó r_e), where w represents market value weights, r represents costs of capital, and t is the tax rate. Calculate cost of debt using YTM or debt rating approach. Calculate cost of preferred stock as preferred dividend divided by price. Calculate cost of equity using CAPM (r_e = R_f + Œ≤(R_m - R_f)), dividend discount model, or bond yield plus risk premium approach. Understand that WACC represents the minimum return required on new investments of average risk. Use market values, not book values, for weights.

LOS 6.b Coming Soon
Explain factors affecting capital structure and the weighted-average cost of capital
Analyze factors influencing capital structure decisions: business risk (operating leverage, revenue volatility), tax considerations (debt tax shield benefits), financial flexibility (preserving borrowing capacity), agency costs (conflicts between stakeholders), asymmetric information (pecking order theory), growth opportunities, and asset characteristics (tangibility affects collateral value). Understand that WACC changes with leverage due to changing costs and weights of debt and equity. Examine how industry characteristics, firm maturity, profitability, and macroeconomic conditions affect optimal leverage. Recognize country-specific factors including legal systems, bankruptcy codes, and tax regimes.

LOS 6.c Coming Soon
Explain the Modigliani‚ÄìMiller propositions regarding capital structure
Understand MM Proposition I: Without taxes, capital structure is irrelevant to firm value (value determined by assets, not how they're financed). With taxes, value increases with leverage due to tax shield (V_L = V_U + (t √ó D)). Understand MM Proposition II: Without taxes, cost of equity increases linearly with leverage (r_e = r_0 + (r_0 - r_d)(D/E)). With taxes, r_e = r_0 + (r_0 - r_d)(D/E)(1-t). Recognize underlying assumptions: perfect capital markets, no taxes/bankruptcy costs/agency costs, and symmetric information. Understand that real-world deviations from these assumptions create trade-offs determining optimal capital structure.

LOS 6.d Coming Soon
Describe optimal and target capital structures
Understand optimal capital structure as the mix that minimizes WACC and maximizes firm value, balancing benefits of debt (tax shields, disciplining management) against costs (financial distress, bankruptcy, agency conflicts, loss of financial flexibility). Recognize static trade-off theory: optimal leverage where marginal benefit of tax shield equals marginal cost of financial distress. Understand target capital structure as management's desired long-term capital mix, which may differ from current structure. Analyze how firms adjust toward targets (rebalancing frequency, adjustment costs). Examine pecking order theory suggesting firms prefer internal financing, then debt, then equity due to information asymmetry costs.
