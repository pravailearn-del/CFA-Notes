Arbitrage, Replication, and the Cost of Carry in Pricing Derivatives

Module Overview: This module introduces the fundamental concepts underlying derivative pricing. You'll learn how arbitrage opportunities drive pricing relationships, how derivatives can be replicated using combinations of other instruments, and how the cost of carry affects the relationship between spot and forward/futures prices.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 4.a Coming Soon
Explain how the concepts of arbitrage and replication are used in pricing derivatives
Understand arbitrage as the simultaneous purchase and sale of equivalent assets to earn risk-free profit. Learn the law of one price: equivalent assets must have the same price, otherwise arbitrage opportunities exist. Explore replication‚Äîcreating a synthetic position that mimics a derivative's payoff using the underlying asset and risk-free borrowing/lending. For example, a forward contract can be replicated by buying the underlying and borrowing at the risk-free rate. These concepts ensure that derivative prices are tied to the prices of underlying assets and prevent persistent arbitrage opportunities.

Explain the difference between the spot and expected future price of an underlying and the cost of carry associated with holding the underlying asset
Distinguish between the spot price (current market price), the expected future price (market's expectation of the future spot price), and the forward/futures price (price agreed today for future delivery). Understand cost of carry: the net cost of holding an asset, including storage costs, financing costs (interest on borrowed funds), and benefits (dividends, interest, or convenience yield). The relationship: Forward Price = Spot Price √ó (1 + Risk-free rate - Income yield + Storage costs).
