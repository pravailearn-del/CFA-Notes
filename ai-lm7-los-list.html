Introduction to Digital Assets

Module Overview: This module introduces digital assets and distributed ledger technology (DLT). Understand blockchain fundamentals, cryptocurrencies, digital tokens, DeFi applications, and the investment characteristics of this emerging asset class.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 7.a Coming Soon
Describe financial applications of distributed ledger technology
Understand blockchain and distributed ledger technology (DLT) fundamentals: decentralization, immutability, transparency, and consensus mechanisms (proof of work, proof of stake). Explore financial applications including cryptocurrencies, tokenization of assets, smart contracts, decentralized finance (DeFi), central bank digital currencies (CBDCs), and settlement systems. Analyze benefits (efficiency, reduced intermediaries, transparency) and challenges (scalability, energy consumption, regulation).

LOS 7.b Coming Soon
Explain investment features of digital assets and contrast them with other asset classes
Examine digital asset categories: cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, altcoins), tokens (utility, security, NFTs), and stablecoins. Understand unique characteristics including 24/7 trading, divisibility, programmability, borderless nature, and disintermediation. Contrast with traditional assets in terms of regulation, custody, valuation methods, volatility, and adoption stage. Analyze the role of digital assets as store of value, medium of exchange, and speculative investments.

LOS 7.c Coming Soon
Describe investment forms and vehicles used in digital asset investments
Explore investment vehicles for digital assets: direct ownership through wallets (hot vs. cold), cryptocurrency exchanges, digital asset funds, exchange-traded products (ETPs), blockchain equity investments, mining operations, and DeFi protocols. Understand custody solutions, security considerations (private keys, multi-signature wallets), regulatory frameworks, and tax implications across different jurisdictions.

LOS 7.d Coming Soon
Analyze sources of risk, return, and diversification among digital asset investments
Evaluate return drivers for digital assets (network adoption, technological innovation, scarcity, speculation) and comprehensive risk factors: extreme volatility, regulatory uncertainty, cybersecurity threats, loss of private keys, exchange failures, market manipulation, liquidity risk, forking events, and technological obsolescence. Analyze diversification potential through correlation with traditional assets, but consider concentration risk and nascent market structure. Understand ESG concerns related to energy consumption.
