Exchange Rate Calculations

Module Overview: This module focuses on foreign exchange calculation mechanics. Mastering currency quotes, cross rates, forward rates, and the relationship between spot rates, forward rates, and interest rates.

Learning Outcome Statements (LOS)

LOS 8.a Coming Soon
Calculate and interpret currency cross-rates
Master cross-rate calculations when direct quotes are unavailable. Understand quote conventions: direct (domestic currency per foreign unit, e.g., INR/USD) versus indirect (foreign currency per domestic unit, e.g., USD/INR). Calculate cross rates using the formula: A/C = (A/B) √ó (B/C) where B is the common currency. For example, to find JPY/AUD given USD/JPY = 110 and USD/AUD = 0.75, calculate AUD/USD = 1/0.75 = 1.333, then JPY/AUD = 110 √ó 1.333 = 146.67. Practice with bid-ask spreads: use appropriate bid or ask rates to ensure no arbitrage opportunities. Understand triangular arbitrage: when cross rate differs from market rate, profit by trading through three currencies.

LOS 8.b Coming Soon
Explain the arbitrage relationship between spot and forward exchange rates and interest rates, calculate a forward rate using points or in percentage terms, and interpret a forward discount or premium
Understand covered interest rate parity (CIRP): F/S = (1 + i_domestic)/(1 + i_foreign), where F is forward rate and S is spot rate. This no-arbitrage condition links spot rates, forward rates, and interest differentials. Calculate forward rates from points: F = S + forward points (divide points by 10,000 for most currencies, 100 for JPY). Calculate in percentage terms: Forward premium/discount = [(F - S)/S] √ó (360/days) √ó 100. Interpret: forward premium (F > S) indicates higher domestic rates; forward discount (F < S) indicates lower domestic rates. Understand that forward rate is NOT a predictor of future spot rate but rather reflects interest rate differential. Practice covered interest arbitrage: if CIRP doesn't hold, borrow in low-rate currency, convert at spot, invest in high-rate currency, lock in forward rate to convert back, and profit from the arbitrage.
